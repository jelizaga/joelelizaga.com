Maligayang Pasko in Manila

BY BIBOT SANTAYANA

 

  
 

Christmas Day was at Peewee's. Everyone came at 1 PM and later because mga ina-antok pa. Eddie was absent because he had asthma and Bambi was on duty. Peewee prepared pancit molo, roast turkey with all the trimmings, rellenong alimasag, fresh lumpia, lasagna, a shrimp salad, fruit salad and cakes. We stayed until 4 PM.

  
 

 

NEW YEAR'S EVE

New Year's Eve was a lot of fun. There were celebrations in different centers of Manila. We decided to go to the Ayala Center street party where a huge stage was set up for programs featuring the big name entertainers. We were in the area of the Peninsula hotel and as early as 5 PM bands were playing and people started coming in. By 7:30 PM the crown was so thick. We had a very good view of the stage and the giant screen. The program was so entertaining and fireworks were set off every 15 minutes. At 20 minutes before midnight, people were all getting so excited, standing on the chairs, making a lot of noise. Finally at midnight, the fireworks display started and went on for 15 minutes. Ang ganda!

Lunch was here at home and everyone started coming past 1 PM because of the New Year's Eve celebrations. We had a singing contest and a "name that tune" contest after the New Year's Day lunch. Ang dami palang disintonado sa pamilya! All the adults including BJ and Jeremy shelled out P50. each which we pooled together and divided for the 1st and 2nd prizes. EVERYONE HAD TO SING. Maryanne Laico kept hiding and when it was her turn, she sang a few lines and gave up. She got P500. from her purse and said, "Magbabayad na lang ako para hindi kumanta." (I'll just pay P500 -- just don't let me sing!)

Everyone was shy about singing. They sang from where they were seated. Mariel was the comic relief. When it was her turn, she went to the front and sang "The Greatest Love of All" with a very heavy Visayan accent, with action and with pakaway-kaway pa, really baduy. She had everyone laughing. She should have been in the Laico reunion.
