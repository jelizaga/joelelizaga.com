December 25, 1999

Do-Re-Mi Karaoke Contest

Perhaps the highlight of the Christmas Day festivities at Popoy and Carol's house, as far as the older set is concerned, besides the feasting, was the karaoke contest. Somehow it reminded me of gladiator combat in ancient Roman times, in which the contestants who were selected to compete didn't have a choice in the matter, let alone choose their opponent. Comparatively, it was against contest rules for the singer to be able to choose the song, and too bad if it wasn't in your key. I don't know if this is what karaoke is like at other places, but I guess it's all part of the fun.

Alfie was the first contestant, singing "Celebration", bringing forth wild cheers from his womenfolk, Helena and Nonon. Next was Grace, singing "Unforgettable". Irresistible, thought husband Arthur, who couldn't keep from planting a kiss on her cheek in midsong. Then, Ricky Martin (Elizaga), veteran karaoke go-er, but unlikely performer, surprised and delighted the crowd with an go-all-out performance of "Twist and Shout", even dancing a charming twist for effect. By this time Jovits and Rory could no longer keep from recruiting themselves as background dancers.

The other Ric, as in Ricardo, sauntered on next, singing "California Girls", to a now wildly cheering crowd. California girlfriend Melissa seemed to heartily approve. I hope Popoy's neighbors will still let him live there-it must have sounded like a stadium. Singing "YMCA" was Paolo, who was surprised to find he instantly had a full row of background dancers supporting him, due to arm motions which the song required. Even though he found out later that he wouldn't have been qualified to be in the running for the prize since his parents were donating it, he still deserves a congrats for the best physical coordination-- spelling, holding a microphone, and singing, all at the same time. Little Kathleen Dizon, bobbing and clapping her hands deserves a prize for Cutest Background Dancer (sorry, Via!).

That was a tough act for Jovits to follow, singing "That's What Friends Are For", but she held her own, abruptly breaking character at the end when she demanded,"Will you still respect me?" The crowd was still hungry for more, and the next victim was Boggie, singing "La Bamba", after trying to abscond by arguing, "I don't speak espaniole." Popoy "The Pretender", was then pressured to the stage by special request from the crowd. And what a show he delivered! See what happens when you peel off all the clinging kids!

Shy-no-more Jovim gets a special mention for his excellent performance of "I Saw Her Standing There". And Malu proved she hasn't lost her touch. She really worked the crowd, singing "I'll Never Fall in Love". After judges Mike, Tito Jun and Sondra conferred, the grand winner of a 5-disc CD player donated by Mitos and Randy, was Ricky Martin (Elizaga), falling on the floor in astonishment. Thanks to all the singers, a good time was had by all in the true Laico tradition.

 

DISAPPOINTED: Rory wanted to be called to sing for the karaoke contest. But not being a contestant didn't stop her from giving her all singing old songs at New Year's Eve.
