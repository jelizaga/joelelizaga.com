The List

1. The hull of Donald Duck's Boat, Miss Daisy, at Mickey's Toontown Fair leaks from every side. Bring your swimsuit and cool off after riding Goofy's Barnstormer rollercoaster. Nothing feels better on those hot Florida afternoons!

2. A table full of Lincoln Logs sits in the middle of a real Lincoln Log fort, Fort Longhorn, on Tom Sawyer's Island in Frontierland in the Magic Kingdom. Build your own fort while under the relentless seige of the "bad guys", then escape through the secret escape tunnel.

3. Babies love It's A Small World. Phoebe rode this ride seven times!

4. Fire hydrants at MGM Studios pulse and spray the corners of New York Street. Don't let the crowds keep you from getting totally soaked. Another good reason to bring your swimsuit!

5. Two trucks carry buckets full of snakes at the Indiana Jones giftshop, MGM studios.

6. The AT-AT walker guarding the entrance to Star Tours, MGM Studios, sprays water from its laser cannons!

7. The real Toy Story Pizza Planet Delivery Truck is parked outside of Pizza Planet, MGM studios.

8. Buzz Lightyear's spaceship has landed outside of Pizza Planet.

9. The Boneyard in Dinoland at Animal Kingdom is everything you've heard of and more! Get there early and start digging for bones at the archaeological dig site. It's harder than it looks - better bring your own shovel!

10. The Sea Serpent waterslide at the Port Orleans Resort makes Doubloon Lagoon the coolest of all the Disney resort pools!

11. The Tiki Gods in Adventureland at the Magic Kingdom spit to the beat of the native drums.

12. Mr. Toad's Wild Ride is gone from Disney World to make room for a new Winnie the Pooh ride, but he lives on at Disneyland. Long Live Mr. Toad!

The E-Rides

Still our all time favorite ride: Splash Mountain. That was then, this is now. Compared with Disneyland, Walt Disney World's Splash Mountain is wetter, has more falls, and a more coherent story line.

Buzz Lightyear's Spaceranger Spin of the new Tomorrowland had a surprise opening at 7:40 on the evening of October 23, 1998 immediately after the SpectroMagic parade. We were there, shooting laser cannons at the Emperor Zurg's evil minions.

The Astro Orbiter of the new Tomorrowland. Imagine the Dumbo ride turbo-charged and on steroids!

The entrance hall of Countdown to Extinction rivals the Smithsonian's Natural History Museum. On the ride monstrous carnivores attack your time travel machine. You can almost feel the heat of their breath as they roar!
