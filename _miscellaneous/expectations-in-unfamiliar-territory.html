Expectations in Unfamiliar Territory

Researcher

The Trackball Experiment

For our INFO 470 - Research Methods class, we devised a simple experiment to determine whether positive or negative reinforcement towards an unfamiliar device (in our case, a trackball) would effect one's performance on a test of precision using said device and one's perception of one's own ability to use said device.

We found a very marginal (insignifcant) correlation between positive reinforcement and our participant's performance on our test, but our sample size was rather small (20 participants) and selected by convenience, so I suspect that such a trend was a matter of chance.
