Tacoman Methanol

A Would-Be Refinery

Around 2016, Northwest Innovation Works caught public controversey over their proposition to construct what would have been the largest methanol refinery on the planet in the Port of Tacoma. This project would be accompanied by two other potential refineries lining the Pacific Northwestern coast. This project was especially divisive given the refinery's impressive blast zone encompassing downtown Tacoma, location within the Mt. Rainier lava flow zone, projected thirst for public utilities, the hazardous pollutants produced by the refining process, and backing by the Chinese government and British Petroleum through a series of subsidiaries.

These refineries would turn liquid natural gas to methanol, which would then be shipped to Dalian, China, where said methanol would be converted to olefinsâ€”hydrocarbons used in the production of plastics.

Funny enough, Northwest Innovation Works cancelled their pursuit of the refinery's construction citing "regulatory issues" before the infographic was even finished.
