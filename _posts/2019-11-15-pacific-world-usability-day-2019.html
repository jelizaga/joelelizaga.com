Puget Sound World Usability Day 2019

  • What: Puget Sound Usability Day 2019
  • When: November 14, 2019
  • Where: University of Washington

I graduated from the University of Washington in the summer of 2017. For the next year I wandered the abyssal professional no-mans-land between 'aspirant UX designer,' 'aspirant developer,' 'aspirant multimedia producer,' 'freelance web-dev & videographer,' 'open source geek,' and 'dear Bathala, someone please interview me so I can finally pin down a single career.' If employment were personal finance, my portfolio was a diversified, tediously sluggish climb through the gig economy before the knuckle-whitening bounces of reversion-to-mean came to resemble linear growth. UX was a hot field with fewer positions in the Seattle metropolitan area than software engineering, with competition further compounded by the influx of professionals from UX-adjacent careers—graphic designers, illustrators, artists, animators, front-end devs, psychologists, creatives, industrial designers, engineers, and the like.

Demonstration of what it's like to finally be a full-time UX designer.

So even months into my employment as a UX designer at Infosys, I had sustained some momentum of incredulity. Am I really here? Am I really a UX designer?

It wasn't until my team of UX folks at the company resolved to attend the annual Puget Sound World Usability Day conference that it hit me:

Whoah.

I've made it.
