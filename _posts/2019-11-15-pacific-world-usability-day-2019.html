Puget Sound World Usability Day 2019

What: Puget Sound Usability Day 2019

When: November 14, 2019

Why: P

I graduated from the University of Washington in the summer of 2017. For the next year I wandered the abyssal professional no-mans-land between 'aspirant UX designer,' 'aspirant developer,' 'aspirant multimedia producer,' 'freelance web-dev & videographer,' 'open source geek,' and 'dear Bathala, someone please interview me so I can finally pin down a single career.' If employment were personal finance, my portfolio was a diversified, tediously sluggish climb through the gig economy before the knuckle-whitening bounces of reversion-to-mean came to resemble linear growth. UX was a hot field with fewer positions in the Seattle metropolitan area than software engineering, competition further compounded by the influx of professionals from UX-adjacent careers—graphic designers, illustrators, artists, animators, front-end devs, psychologists, creatives, industrial designers, engineers, and the like.

So when my team of UX folks at Infosys resolved to attend the annual Puget Sound World Usability Day conference and I picked up my badge from the student volunteers at my alma mater, that was when it hit me:

Whoah.

I've made it.

In 2016, I would've been one of the volunteers handing out badges for the discounted conference attendance. I had a portfolio of class projects and a sense of imposter syndrome
